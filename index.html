2-Year Roadmap

Your Month-by-Month Plan from Class 11 to NEET Day

Most students waste their Class 11 year doing school portions and nothing else. What follows is the right way to use both years.

1
Class 11 · June to September

Build Your Foundation Without Rushing

Class 11 chapters form roughly 45 percent of the NEET paper. This is not the time to rush, it is the time to understand. Physics Mechanics, Organic Chemistry basics, Cell Biology and Genetics are your starting priorities. One chapter per subject per week is a sustainable pace.

2
Class 11 · October to January

Complete Class 11 Syllabus and Start Mock Practice

By January you should have covered all Class 11 chapters. Start sitting for subject-wise mock tests, not full-length ones yet. The goal is to identify which chapters are weak before you get to Class 12, so you can revisit them rather than carry them forward as problems.

3
Class 12 · April to August

Cover Class 12 and Begin Integration

Class 12 chapters are not harder than Class 11, just different. Reproduction, Biotechnology, Modern Physics, Electrochemistry, and Coordination Compounds need proper attention. By August, start taking full-length mock tests and analysing where your scores are low.

4
Class 12 · September to April 2027

Revision Cycles and Mock Test Analysis

Final eight months should have three revision cycles, at least twenty full-length mocks, and detailed PYQ solving. Your mentor should be tracking your progress weekly and adjusting your revision plan based on where your scores are dropping.
