NEET 2027 Syllabus Breakdown — High-Yield Chapters and What Changed

📅 June 2026 ⏱ 8 min read 🎯 NEET 2027

NEET 2027 syllabus follows the NMC guidelines aligned with the revised NCERT 2023-24 curriculum. If you're starting preparation now, this matters: some chapters shifted between classes and some content was restructured. Here's exactly what carries the most marks and what you can safely de-prioritize.

Biology — The 360-Mark Subject

Biology is 50% of your score. Treat it that way. Class 11 Biology contributes roughly 45% of NEET Biology questions; Class 12 gives 55%. The highest-yield chapters:

  • Genetics & Evolution (Chapters 5-7, Class 12) — 12-18 questions every year. Mendelian laws, chromosomal theory, molecular basis of inheritance.
  • Human Physiology (Chapters 17-22, Class 11) — 10-14 questions. Digestion, breathing, body fluids, locomotion, neural control, chemical coordination.
  • Plant Physiology (Chapters 11-15, Class 11) — 8-12 questions. Mineral nutrition, photosynthesis, respiration, plant growth.
  • Reproduction (Chapters 1-4, Class 12) — 8-10 questions. Flowering plants + human reproduction.
  • Cell Biology (Chapters 8-10, Class 11) — 7-10 questions. Cell structure, biomolecules, cell cycle.
⚡ NCERT Line Matters

In NEET 2023 and 2024, over 70% of Biology questions were directly from NCERT text — not just diagrams, but specific sentences. Read every word, including footnotes and box items.

Chemistry — The Balance Subject

Chemistry is 180 marks divided roughly 40% Physical, 35% Organic, 25% Inorganic. Most students over-invest in Organic and under-prepare Inorganic. Fix this imbalance:

  • Organic Chemistry (Class 12, Chapters 10-16): Alcohol, Aldehydes, Carboxylic Acids, Amines are 6-9 questions combined.
  • Physical Chemistry (Class 11+12): Chemical Kinetics, Electrochemistry, Thermodynamics — calculation-based, high scoring if practiced.
  • Inorganic Chemistry: p-Block and d-Block elements, Coordination Compounds. Pure memory — revise weekly or you'll forget.

Physics — The Differentiator

Physics separates 580 scorers from 650+ scorers. Most students score 80-100/180 in Physics; toppers score 140+. The gap is numerical practice, not concept reading.

  • Mechanics (Class 11, Chapters 2-9): Kinematics, Laws of Motion, Work-Energy, Rotational Motion — 20-25 questions.
  • Electrostatics + Current Electricity (Class 12, Chapters 1-3): 12-16 questions. Heavy calculation component.
  • Optics + Modern Physics (Class 12, Chapters 9-14): 10-14 questions. Modern Physics is formula-driven — easier to score.
