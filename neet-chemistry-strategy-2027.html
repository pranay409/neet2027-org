NEET Chemistry 2027 — Complete Subject Strategy for 160+ Marks

📅 June 2026 ⏱ 8 min read 🎯 NEET 2027

Chemistry is NEET's most balanced subject — equally split between memory (Inorganic), understanding (Physical), and application (Organic). Students who treat it as a single subject fail; students who build three distinct approaches for its three branches consistently score 140-160+. Here's the strategy.

Physical Chemistry — Master the Calculations

Physical Chemistry questions are calculation-heavy but formula-predictable. If you know the formula and can plug in numbers correctly, you get the mark. There's less ambiguity here than in Organic or Inorganic. Key chapters:

  • Chemical Kinetics (Class 12, Ch 4): Rate laws, half-life, Arrhenius equation — 3-5 questions every year
  • Electrochemistry (Class 12, Ch 3): Nernst equation, Faraday's laws, EMF calculations — 3-4 questions
  • Thermodynamics (Class 11, Ch 6): ΔH, ΔG, entropy — 3-5 questions, conceptual + numerical
  • Solutions (Class 12, Ch 2): Colligative properties, molarity/molality — 2-4 questions
  • Equilibrium (Class 11, Ch 7): Kp, Kc, Le Chatelier — 3-5 questions
📐 Physical Chemistry Rule

Make a formula sheet for Physical Chemistry covering all equations from the chapters above. Revise this sheet every Sunday. Physical Chemistry rewards those who know formulas instantly — not those who derive them under pressure.

Organic Chemistry — Mechanism Over Memorisation

The biggest Organic Chemistry mistake: memorising reactions without understanding mechanisms. NEET Organic questions increasingly test mechanism reasoning — "which product is formed" or "which reagent is needed" — not just "name this reaction." Understand electron movement, nucleophile/electrophile interactions, and leaving group ability.

High-yield Organic chapters: Alcohols, Phenols, Ethers (Class 12, Ch 11); Aldehydes, Ketones (Class 12, Ch 12); Amines (Class 12, Ch 13); Haloalkanes (Class 12, Ch 10). These 5 chapters typically yield 12-16 questions.

Inorganic Chemistry — The Memory Game

Inorganic is pure memory. Most students hate it, which means it's where consistent performers separate themselves. You don't need to understand why BrF5 has a T-shaped geometry — you need to know it and be able to recall it in 30 seconds during the exam. Tools that work:

  • Mnemonics for periodic trends and exceptions
  • Flashcards for oxidation states, hybridisation, colours of compounds
  • Weekly revision of Inorganic notes — without weekly revision, it fades fast

High-yield: p-Block Elements (Class 12, Ch 7): 8-12 questions. d-Block + Coordination Compounds (Class 12, Ch 8-9): 6-10 questions.

Balancing the Three Branches

Recommended time split: Physical 30%, Organic 35%, Inorganic 35%. Most aspirants over-invest in Organic and under-revise Inorganic. The result: they score 25-30/45 in Inorganic when they could be scoring 35-40 with proper weekly revision.
